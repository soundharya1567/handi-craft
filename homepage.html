Earth & Hand: A Gallery of Indian Craft Souls

Combines natural origin with human touch — earthy and emotional

Bamboo Craft

These baskets are woven in Assam using locally harvested bamboo. Artisans use hand-cut strips and traditional weaving techniques passed down through generations. Tools include handmade knives and wooden frames sourced from village blacksmiths.

Block Printing

Originating from Bagru, Rajasthan, this textile is printed using teak wood blocks carved by master craftsmen. Natural dyes are prepared from turmeric, indigo, and pomegranate peels. The cloth is pre-washed in river water and sun-dried before printing.

Woodwork

These panels are carved in Saharanpur, Uttar Pradesh, using rosewood and sheesham. Tools include chisels, mallets, and hand planes made by local toolmakers. Each design is sketched by hand and carved with precision over several days. The wood is sourced from sustainably managed forests near the foothills of the Himalayas. Artisans often work in family-run workshops, where skills are passed down through generations. Before carving begins, the wood is seasoned for weeks to prevent warping. Each panel reflects not just craftsmanship, but the cultural motifs of Mughal and Rajasthani architecture.

Embroidery

This vibrant embroidery is crafted in Kutch, Gujarat. Threads are dyed using natural pigments, and the fabric is stretched on wooden frames made by village carpenters. Each motif tells a story, stitched using needles sourced from local markets.

Jewelry Making

Crafted in Tamil Nadu, this gold jewelry is shaped using tiny anvils and hammers forged by traditional metalsmiths. Gold is sourced from local traders and purified in-house. Designs are inspired by temple architecture and mythology.

Leather Craft

These wallets and belts are handmade in Rajasthan using camel leather. The leather is tanned using natural bark and oils. Stitching tools like prongs and mallets are crafted by local toolmakers. Each piece is burnished and polished by hand.

Metal Engraving

This engraved bowl is made in Moradabad, Uttar Pradesh. Brass sheets are shaped and etched using chisels and hammers. Designs are inspired by Mughal motifs. Tools are custom-made by engraving families who’ve practiced for centuries.

Pottery Making

These clay pots are crafted in Khurja, Uttar Pradesh. Clay is sourced from riverbeds and refined manually. Pottery wheels are built from wood and iron, and pigments are made from minerals and ash. Each pot is sun-dried and kiln-fired.The process begins with artisans kneading the clay by foot to achieve the perfect consistency. Each pot is shaped with care, often guided by muscle memory and years of experience. Once formed, the pots are painted with natural colors inspired by local festivals, flora, and folklore. Firing takes place in traditional brick kilns fueled by dried leaves and twigs, giving the pottery its signature earthy finish. These handcrafted pieces are sold in village haats and urban craft fairs, connecting rural tradition with modern appreciation.
