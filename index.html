Earth & Hand: A Gallery of Indian Craft Souls

Combines natural origin with human touch — earthy and emotional

Bamboo Craft

These baskets are woven in Assam using locally harvested bamboo. Artisans use hand-cut strips and traditional weaving techniques passed down through generations. Tools include handmade knives and wooden frames sourced from village blacksmiths.

Block Printing

Originating from Bagru, Rajasthan, this textile is printed using teak wood blocks carved by master craftsmen. Natural dyes are prepared from turmeric, indigo, and pomegranate peels. The cloth is pre-washed in river water and sun-dried before printing.

Woodwork

These panels are carved in Saharanpur, Uttar Pradesh, using rosewood and sheesham. Tools include chisels, mallets, and hand planes made by local toolmakers. Each design is sketched by hand and carved with precision over several days. The wood is sourced from sustainably managed forests near the foothills of the Himalayas.

Embroidery

This vibrant embroidery is crafted in Kutch, Gujarat. Threads are dyed using natural pigments, and the fabric is stretched on wooden frames made by village carpenters. Each motif tells a story.

Jewelry Making

Crafted in Tamil Nadu, this gold jewelry is shaped using tiny anvils and hammers forged by traditional metalsmiths. Designs are inspired by temple architecture and mythology.

Leather Craft

These wallets and belts are handmade in Rajasthan using camel leather. The leather is tanned using natural bark and oils.

Metal Engraving

This engraved bowl is made in Moradabad, Uttar Pradesh. Brass sheets are shaped and etched using chisels and hammers. Designs are inspired by Mughal motifs.

Pottery Making

These clay pots are crafted in Khurja, Uttar Pradesh. Clay is sourced from riverbeds and refined manually. Pottery wheels are built from wood and iron, and pigments are made from minerals and ash.
